Melbourne's Busiest Train Stations

Melbourne's train network is a vital part of the city’s public transport system, serving thousands of commuters every day. As the city grows, understanding which train stations experience the highest ridership is crucial for ensuring that resources are efficiently allocated and services meet demand. This visualisation aims to provide insights into the busiest train stations across different lines in Melbourne.

The graph on the right displays the total yearly passengers in Melbourne over several years. Ridership was relatively high during FY18-19, peaking before a significant decline due to the COVID-19 pandemic, This drop reflects the impact of pandemic-related restrictions, such as lockdowns and an increase in remote work. In subsequent years, ridership shows a steady recovery, though it has not yet reached pre-pandemic levels.

Where are people going?

From the map below, it is evident that the busiest train stations in Melbourne are primarily located within the CBD; Colloquially known as the City Loop Stations. This concentration of activity is largely due to the CBD being the city’s economic and commercial hub, where a vast number of offices, retail stores, and entertainment venues are located.

Outside of City Loop Stations, the most busy stations are those served as a hub for multiple train lines, where its the last shared station before individual services go to their exclusive stations. An example would be Dandenong where it is the last shared station before Pakenham and Cranbourne services.
